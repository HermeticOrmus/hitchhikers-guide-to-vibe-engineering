# Code of Conduct

> *"It is a mistake to think you can solve any major problems just with potatoes."*
>
> *It is also a mistake to think you can build a community without some basic guidelines.*

---

## The One Rule

**Don't be a Conditions of Relative Conditions of Relative Difficulty Inducer.**

Or in simpler terms: **Don't be a jerk.**

---

## The Slightly Longer Version

### Be Kind
The universe is vast, cold, and mostly empty. This community doesn't have to be. Treat others as you'd want to be treated after a long journey through hyperspace.

### Be Patient
Not everyone knows what you know. Some travelers are just starting their journey with AI-assisted development. Guide them; don't gatekeep.

### Be Constructive
Criticism is welcome. Cruelty is not. If you think something could be better, explain how. "This is wrong" helps no one. "This could be improved by..." helps everyone.

---

## What We Won't Tolerate

- Harassment of any kind
- Discrimination based on identity
- Trolling, insults, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information
- Being the kind of person who makes the universe slightly worse

---

## Enforcement

Violations may result in:
1. A polite reminder (we assume good intent first)
2. A warning
3. Temporary ban
4. Permanent ban (reserved for truly Conditions of Relative Conditions of Relative Difficulty)

---

## Reporting

If someone is making you uncomfortable, contact the maintainers. We take this seriously.

---

## In Summary

> *"A common mistake that people make when trying to design something completely foolproof is to underestimate the ingenuity of complete fools."*

We can't design a foolproof community. But we can agree to be kind to each other while we figure out this whole AI-assisted development thing together.

**DON'T PANIC. And don't be a jerk.**

---

*This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including the repository, discussions, issues, and any associated communication channels.*
